# AI Bubble / Reality Check — Live Research

Ongoing project tracking the state of the AI market, bubble dynamics, and whether the current situation is sustainable. Not a prediction — a watching brief.

## Core Thesis

> "I would not expect a clean 'dot-com style pop' on a specific date. If there *is* an AI bubble unwind, it's more likely to happen in layers, with the first serious crack risk in late 2026 to 2027, not as a single Hollywood crater."

### Layer breakdown

| Layer | Bubble Risk | Vulnerable Window | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Software / private AI startups | Highest | Now through 2027 | Most bubble-like, thinnest moats |
| AI infrastructure stocks/suppliers | Moderate | Correction if hyperscaler spending slows | Underlying demand more real than late-90s |
| Memory / HBM / datacenter buildout | Low | Probably not first | Attached to actual capacity bottlenecks and revenue |

## Scenarios

### A — Valuation correction + startup massacre (60%)
- 2026-2027: public AI re-rates downward, private funding gets harsher
- Infrastructure hit if capex growth slows
- Real winners survive and consolidate

### B — Bigger bust from overbuild (25%)
- 2027-2028: too many datacenters, too much inventory ordered on peak assumptions
- Enterprise demand doesn't justify it fast enough
- Closest to a dot-com-style washout

### C — Messy rotation, no dramatic bust (15%)
- AI stays expensive, capex growth slows but remains high
- Returns concentrate into platform/infrastructure winners
- Everything else quietly repriced

## Tripwires to Watch
